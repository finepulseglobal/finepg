Privacy Policy

How FinePulse handles personal data across freight, procurement, shipping, support, and website interactions.

Effective June 23, 2026. Applies to website visitors, customers, vendors, and shipment contacts.

This Privacy Policy explains how FinePulse Global Logistics Limited ("FinePulse", "we", "our", or "us") collects and uses personal information when you visit our website, request a quote, book logistics services, communicate with our team, or appear as a shipper, consignee, vendor, emergency contact, or authorized representative in shipment documentation.

We operate internationally, so this policy is written to support common privacy expectations under laws such as the Nigeria Data Protection Act, GDPR and UK GDPR principles, California-style privacy rights, and other comparable data protection frameworks. Where a local law gives you stronger rights, we will honor those rights where they apply.

1. Scope and Accountability

FinePulse acts as a data controller for information we collect to manage our website, quote requests, customer relationships, billing, compliance, marketing, and business administration. In some logistics workflows, we may act as a processor or service provider when handling data strictly on behalf of a customer, freight partner, carrier, customs broker, or platform provider.

This policy does not replace any written logistics contract, bill of lading, carrier terms, customs declaration, data processing agreement, or service-level agreement. If there is a conflict, the signed service document governs the specific shipment or account.

2. Personal Information We Collect

We collect information that is reasonably necessary to quote, book, move, track, insure, clear, deliver, and support shipments.

  • Identity and contact details: name, company, job title, email, phone number, billing address, delivery address, shipper and consignee contacts.
  • Shipment and trade information: origin, destination, cargo description, value, dimensions, weight, packaging, tracking number, purchase order, customs documents, tax identification where required, and delivery instructions.
  • Account and billing information: invoices, payment status, transaction references, account preferences, contract terms, and credit-related business information.
  • Website and device information: IP address, browser type, pages visited, form submissions, approximate location from device signals, cookie identifiers, and analytics events.
  • Communications: emails, call notes, chat messages, quote requests, complaints, support tickets, survey responses, and consent records.
  • Compliance information: sanctions screening results, anti-fraud checks, export-control documentation, customs records, and legally required audit logs.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal information unless it is necessary for a shipment, legal obligation, safety issue, employment process, or another specifically disclosed purpose.

3. How and Why We Use Personal Information

We use personal information under lawful bases such as contract performance, legitimate business interests, consent, legal obligation, vital interests, and protection of legal claims.

  • To prepare freight quotes, plan routes, arrange collection, manage customs clearance, and deliver cargo.
  • To verify customers, vendors, shipment parties, payment status, and compliance requirements.
  • To communicate shipment updates, service notices, billing messages, operational issues, and support responses.
  • To improve website performance, measure service demand, secure our systems, and troubleshoot technical issues.
  • To send marketing or service updates where permitted by law and to respect opt-out preferences.
  • To prevent fraud, comply with sanctions, customs, tax, transport, aviation, maritime, courier, and safety obligations.

4. Sharing, International Transfers, and Service Providers

Global logistics requires controlled sharing. We may share data with carriers, airlines, ocean lines, trucking partners, warehouses, customs brokers, courier partners, insurers, payment providers, IT vendors, analytics providers, legal advisers, regulators, and government agencies where needed for service delivery or compliance.

Shipment information may be transferred across borders, including to countries that may not have the same privacy laws as your country. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards such as contractual commitments, transfer impact reviews, data minimization, access controls, and partner due diligence.

FinePulse does not sell personal information as a business model. If a law defines certain analytics or advertising activities as a "sale" or "sharing", eligible users may opt out by contacting us.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, relationship with us, and applicable law, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a copy of your personal information. You may also have the right to withdraw consent, opt out of marketing, opt out of certain sharing, lodge a complaint with a regulator, or appeal our decision on a rights request.

To protect shipment integrity and other parties, we may need to verify your identity and authority before fulfilling a request. We may also retain information where required for customs, tax, transport, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, safety, or legal obligations.

6. Security, Retention, Cookies, and Children

Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information, including role-based access, secure document handling, vendor controls, staff training, and monitoring for misuse. No website or logistics network can be guaranteed completely secure, but we work to reduce risk and respond quickly to incidents.

Retention

We keep personal information only as long as necessary for the purpose collected, including shipment performance, legal compliance, accounting, customs audits, insurance, dispute resolution, and legitimate business records. Retention periods vary by document type and applicable law.

Cookies

Our website may use essential cookies, analytics technologies, and similar tools to operate the site, understand performance, improve user experience, and protect forms from abuse. Browser settings may allow you to block or delete cookies, but some features may not work properly.

Children

Our services are intended for businesses and adults. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children for marketing or account creation.

7. Contact and Updates

Questions, rights requests, or privacy complaints may be sent to Contact via request form or by writing to FinePulse Global Logistics Limited, Location shared upon request.

We may update this Privacy Policy as our services, laws, or operations change. The effective date above shows when this version became active.
