Our Story

From a Single Route
to Every Continent

FinePulse began in Lagos with a practical belief that international shipping should be clearer, faster, and more accountable for growing businesses.

Our founders identified a simple gap in everyday trade: growing businesses needed clearer pricing, stronger follow-through, and better communication after goods left the supplier. FinePulse was built to close that gap with practical freight coordination, disciplined documentation, and reliable carrier relationships.

Today, FinePulse supports importers, exporters, SMEs, retailers, procurement teams, and enterprises moving cargo across Nigeria and major global trade lanes, with every shipment handled through accountable communication from quote to delivery.

FinePulse operates across all four primary freight modes: road, air, sea, and rail. For most international shipments, we combine modes to optimise for time, cost, and carbon impact. Our multi-modal capability means you receive a single invoice, a single point of contact, and real-time tracking regardless of how many modes your cargo moves through.

Submit your shipment details through our contact form or call our freight desk directly. We return a fully itemised quote within two business hours for standard lanes, and within four hours for complex or oversized freight. Quotes include all known surcharges — no hidden fees added at invoicing.

All active shipments are visible through the FinePulse client portal, updated every 15 minutes via GPS and carrier data feeds. You can share tracking links with your own customers, configure automated milestone alerts via email or SMS, and access a full event log from pickup to delivery. We support the full documentation lifecycle for commercial shipments, including invoices, packing lists, bills of lading, certificates of origin, permits where required, and customs handoff coordination. For recurring trade lanes, we help clients prepare documentation earlier so clearance risks are reduced before cargo arrives.

FinePulse works with qualified carriers, licensed customs partners, and documented operating procedures appropriate to each shipment type. Where a cargo category requires special handling, carrier certification, temperature control, or regulated documentation, we confirm those requirements during quote and booking before the shipment moves.

Cargo insurance availability depends on the shipment value, commodity, route, carrier terms, and declared risk profile. We can help arrange suitable cargo cover through approved insurance partners when requested, and we confirm coverage terms before booking so clients understand limits, exclusions, and required documentation.
